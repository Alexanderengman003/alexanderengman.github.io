About the company

Ascilion designs, develops, and manufactures products based on hollow microneedles for sampling of dermal interstitial fluid (dISF) from the skin. By using state-of-the-art MEMS (micro electromechanical systems) technology, the company is able to offer a unique micro-needle based sampling solution.

Integration of dISF extraction tool in customer applications

Work description
After three years of intensive development at Ascilion, I successfully led the product from its design phase to completion. Regulatory compliance achievements
Leveraging my in-depth knowledge of the product's development, I took charge of ensuring compliance with key industry standards. I successfully managed the compliance process for IEC 60601 (electrical safety) and IEC 62304 (software life cycle processes). As the project manager for IEC 60601 compliance, I orchestrated rigorous design and safety testing, maintaining continuous dialogue with testing bodies and expertly managing a complex web of requirements. This process involved coordinating with multiple departments within Ascilion, external consultants, and regulatory bodies. I had to ensure that every aspect of our product, from its electrical components to its software architecture, met the exacting standards required for medical devices. This often meant revisiting and refining aspects of our design, a process that required both technical expertise and diplomatic skills to balance regulatory requirements with practical considerations. A major accomplishment was developing a comprehensive risk management plan, which became a cornerstone of our regulatory strategy. This involved a systematic analysis of potential risks associated with our device, from electrical hazards to software malfunctions, and developing mitigation strategies for each. The plan I created not only satisfied regulatory requirements but also significantly enhanced the safety and reliability of our product. On the software front, I led the effort to align our product with IEC 62304 standards, developing an exhaustive test plan and detailed requirements specification that significantly enhanced our product's reliability and market readiness. This involved a complete overhaul of our software development processes, implementing rigorous documentation practices, and establishing a robust system for tracking and resolving software issues throughout the product lifecycle.

Business development and customer relations

Development of medical device for extraction of liquid dISF

Work description
In my initial years at Ascilion, I played a pivotal role in transforming a prototype medical device into a market-ready product suitable for clinical applications. My contributions were instrumental in both hardware and software development, showcasing my versatility and technical expertise. This role required me to rapidly acquire new skills and knowledge, often venturing into unfamiliar territories of medical device development.

Hardware development achievements
I spearheaded the development of sophisticated test setups for various components, ranging from mechanical testing of silicon features to advanced optical inspection systems. A significant achievement was designing and building custom hardware solutions, often integrating microcontroller systems for automation and PC-enabled operator interactions. This work demonstrated my ability to bridge theoretical concepts with practical, real-world applications. One of my key projects involved developing a novel microneedle testing apparatus. This required me to design a system that could accurately measure the penetration force and structural integrity of our silicon microneedles, which are critical components of our dISF extraction device. I integrated force sensors, high-precision actuators, and a custom-designed fixture to hold the microneedles. The resulting system not only improved our quality control process but also provided valuable data for optimizing our microneedle design. Another major hardware development was a custom fluidic testing platform. This system was designed to simulate the extraction of interstitial fluid under various conditions, allowing us to optimize our device's performance across different use cases. I incorporated microfluidic components, precision pumps, and environmental controls to create a versatile testing environment. This platform became an essential tool in our R&D process, significantly accelerating our product development cycle.

Software development innovations
My software development efforts were crucial in advancing our lab capabilities and product functionality. I developed a suite of software tools using SQL, C#, C++, and Python, showcasing my polyglot programming skills. A major accomplishment was creating the main GUI for Ascilion's dISF extraction tool PELSA, which significantly enhanced user experience and device control. This software not only facilitated data acquisition and analysis but also integrated seamlessly with physical systems, demonstrating my ability to create cohesive hardware-software solutions. The PELSA GUI was a particularly challenging project, requiring me to design an intuitive interface that could be used by both researchers and clinical staff. I implemented real-time data visualization, automated calibration routines, and a comprehensive error handling system. The software also included a data management module that securely stored and organized patient data, complying with relevant medical data protection regulations. In addition to the GUI, I developed a suite of data analysis tools that automated much of our research workflow. This included algorithms for processing raw sensor data, identifying key biomarkers, and generating comprehensive reports. These tools not only increased our research efficiency but also provided valuable insights that guided our product refinement process.

Additional key contributions:
One of my standout achievements was designing and implementing a fully automated optical inspection tool, which became the cornerstone of our quality assurance process. This project involved complex programming, including a 4-axis stage system and real-time image analysis, showcasing my skills in automation and computer vision. The system could detect micron-level defects in our silicon microneedles, ensuring consistently high product quality and significantly reducing our rejection rate. I also made significant contributions to experimental design, particularly in microfluidics and biology, broadening the application scope of our technology. For instance, I designed a series of experiments to optimize the extraction of specific biomarkers from interstitial fluid, which involved collaborating closely with our biology team and external research partners. These experiments not only improved our device's performance but also opened up new potential applications in fields like sports medicine and personalized nutrition. My expertise in silicon wafer inspection, using techniques like SEM and LOM, was crucial in maintaining high product quality. I took the initiative to design and manufacture customized tools for these inspections, demonstrating my problem-solving skills and hands-on approach. One such tool was a custom stage for our SEM that allowed for high-throughput inspection of microneedle arrays, significantly speeding up our quality control process. Beyond technical development, I took on responsibilities in project management, product design, documentation, and customer support, showcasing my versatility and ability to manage multiple aspects of product development and deployment. I led several cross-functional teams, coordinating efforts between our engineering, biology, and manufacturing departments to ensure smooth product development and timely delivery.

What I learned in this role:
This role was a transformative experience, significantly expanding my technical and professional skill set. I gained comprehensive knowledge in medical device development, from initial concept to market-ready product. My self-taught journey in software development, particularly in creating complex, integrated systems, was a major personal achievement. The experience in CAD (Solidworks), 3D printing, and microfluidics broadened my expertise in cutting-edge manufacturing techniques. Working in a startup environment honed my ability to manage multiple responsibilities efficiently and make critical decisions in a fast-paced setting. I learned to prioritize tasks effectively, balance short-term deliverables with long-term goals, and adapt quickly to changing project requirements. This role also deepened my understanding of the interdisciplinary nature of medical device development. I learned to effectively communicate and collaborate with professionals from diverse backgrounds, including engineers, biologists, regulatory experts, and clinicians. This experience has made me a more well-rounded professional, capable of bridging the gap between different specialties and driving innovation in complex, multidisciplinary projects.
